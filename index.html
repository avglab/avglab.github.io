About the Lab

Where microbiology meets vascular surgery

The Gaines Laboratory at the University of Chicago Department of Surgery investigates the molecular and microbial mechanisms driving arteriovenous fistula (AVF) failure in hemodialysis patients.

We combine microbiome engineering, targeted nanomedicine, and human tissue platforms to develop disease-modifying therapies at the intersection of vascular biology, nephrology, and synthetic biology.

Led by Dr. "Mimi" Sara Gaines, the lab operates within a collaborative environment at UChicago, with close ties to the Duchossois Family Institute and microbiome engineering collaborators.

Research Focus

Arteriovenous fistula failure, uremic toxins, gut microbiome engineering, targeted nanomedicine, endothelial mesenchymal transition, and ex vivo vascular platforms.

Research

Three programs, one mission

AVF failure is driven by neointimal hyperplasia, a process fueled by uremic toxins, endothelial injury, and smooth muscle cell dysregulation. We attack this problem from three complementary angles.
